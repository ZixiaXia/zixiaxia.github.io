About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine, advised by Prof. Marco Levorato.

My research focuses on improving the reliability of embodied AI systems by building structured world representations. I work on integrating geometry, uncertainty, and multi-sensor information to support tasks such as navigation, SLAM, and 3D reconstruction. My experience includes BEV-based perception, multi-sensor fusion (cameras, lidars, radars), reinforcement learning, and vision-language-action models.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Computer Science from Tianjin University, advised by Prof. Gang Pan, and completed internships at KLA Corporation, Qcraft, and Microsoft.
